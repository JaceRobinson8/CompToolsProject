Overview

Politicians and Super Tuesday in 140 Characters or Less

Super Tuesday is when the greatest number of U.S. states holds primary elections. Super Tuesday this year was on March 1st. This important day determines the early frontrunners and generally causes the biggest losers from each political party to drop out of the race. This year the Republican primaries have received extra attention due to the ‘vocal’ candidate Donald Trump. Social media has been a hugely popular tool to communicate political ideas to others for many years. Twitter is a particularly popular social media source due to its convenience and simplicity. An interesting question I will examine in this report is how much meaningful information is actually available on Twitter? Specifically, what can Twitter tell us about Super Tuesday candidates, voters, and winners? Can we generate some characteristics of a candidate and their voters on solely on Twitter without needing outside knowledge? Through analysis of large data samples available on Twitter, I seek to summarize the current political landscape.

Data

Twitter functions through user tweets, retweets, follows, and hashtags. A tweet is a 140 characters or less message, an image, or a short video uploaded by a user. A retweet is when one user forwards another user’s tweet to all his or her followers. When a user follows another user, he or she is able to see all tweets and retweets of that user. Through the use of hashtags, the symbol ‘#’ and a message, users are able to connect through specific user defined moments. Through these four primary functions, Twitter has grown to have 320 million active users per month. This social network has become one of the central sources for mass information on global events. Famous actors, presidential candidates, local celebrities and your average Joe are all connected on Twitter.

I hypothesize Twitter metrics such as number of retweets, number of followers, and daily activity of a candidate will positively correlate with success of a candidate.
